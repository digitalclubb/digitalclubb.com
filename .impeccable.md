## Design Context

### Users
General professional audience: hiring managers evaluating engineering leadership, peers and collaborators in the industry, and potential clients for consulting or contract work. They arrive looking for evidence of deep technical expertise and clear thinking. Context is typically desktop or mobile browsing during research — quick scanning, then deep reading of specific pieces.

### Brand Personality
**Calm, sharp, credible.** Understated confidence that lets the work speak. No performative energy, no self-promotion beyond the substance. The site should feel like talking to someone who has done the work and doesn't need to convince you of it.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Technical / precise.** Engineering-flavored — structured, data-informed, systematic. Think well-organised documentation rather than a creative portfolio. Strong typographic hierarchy, generous whitespace, clear information architecture. The design itself should demonstrate the same qualities Gareth writes about: reliability, maintainability, and attention to quality.

- **Theme**: Light mode primary with dark mode via `prefers-color-scheme`. Warm stone neutrals.
- **Typography**: Inter (sans), Newsreader (serif), JetBrains Mono (code). Fluid clamp() scale.
- **Anti-references**: Flashy portfolios with animations, gradient text, glassmorphism, hero metrics layouts. Nothing that says "look at my site" over "read my work."

### Design Principles
1. **Content over chrome** — Every element earns its place. If it doesn't help the reader, remove it.
2. **Precision signals competence** — Consistent spacing, aligned elements, and correct semantic markup demonstrate the same engineering values the content describes.
3. **Readable at every scale** — Fluid typography, logical flow, and proper contrast. Works on a phone in sunlight and a monitor at midnight.
4. **Quiet confidence** — No need to shout. Strong hierarchy and good writing do the work. Avoid decorative elements that don't serve comprehension.
5. **Accessible by default** — WCAG AA compliance. Keyboard navigation, screen reader support, reduced motion respect, semantic HTML.
